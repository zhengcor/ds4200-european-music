European Music Trends

Joyce Lee, Oliver Baccay, Corey Zheng

Music is a universal language, but the way people listen varies from country to country. For this project, we explored Spotify's top-charting data across 10 European countries to investigate a central question: How does geographical location in Europe influence the distribution of music genres and audio features?

Using a collection of interactive visualizations, we analyze characteristics like danceability, energy, valence, tempo, and more to uncover how regional culture and geography shape musical preferences. Below, you'll find an introduction to our dataset, links to each visualization, and our conclusions.

Introduction to the Data

We are obtaining our data from the ‘Top Spotify Songs in 73 Countries’ dataset found on Kaggle, which tracks daily Spotify Top 50 charts for different countries. The original CSV file has a total of 2,110,316 rows and 25 columns, with each row representing a different song and each column representing a different metadata or audio feature. We plan to clean our data to get rid of unnecessary categories and reduce our scope to ten European countries that are dominant in the music industry: United Kingdom, Germany, France, Spain, Italy, Netherlands, Sweden, Poland, Belgium, and Portugal. The categories that we will use from the database are artists, country, popularity, is_explicit, duration_ms, danceability, energy, key, loudness, mode, speechless, acousticness, instrumentalness, liveness, valence, tempo, and time_signature. While some categories need no elaboration, others may warrant a small explanation here. For instance, the key category has 12 values corresponding to the possible tonic notes, 0-11, and is categorical. Mode is binary and signals if it is in a major key or minor key. Most of the other categories, such as acousticness, instrumentalness, liveness, and valence are measured in values between 0 and 1 as a numerical score. After data cleaning, our data will display the top 500 songs from each country, with the dataframe totalling 5000 rows and 17 columns.
