Welcome to European Music Trends

Music is a universal language, but the way people listen varies from country to country. For this project, we explored Spotify's top-charting data across 10 European countries to investigate a central question: How does geographical location in Europe influence the distribution of music genres and audio features?

Using a collection of interactive visualizations, we analyze characteristics like danceability, energy, valence, tempo, and more to uncover how regional culture and geography shape musical preferences. Below, you'll find an introduction to our dataset, links to each visualization, and our conclusions.

Introduction to the Dataset

Our data comes from the Top Spotify Songs in 73 Countries dataset on Kaggle. From the full dataset, we filtered to the top 500 songs in each of 10 European countries — the United Kingdom, Germany, France, Spain, Italy, Netherlands, Sweden, Poland, Belgium, and Portugal — giving us 5,000 tracks to analyze.

Each song includes a variety of audio features provided by Spotify's audio analysis API:

  • Danceability — How suitable a track is for dancing
  • Energy — Intensity and activity level of the track
  • Valence — Musical positiveness (happy vs. sad)
  • Acousticness — Likelihood the track is acoustic
  • Speechiness — Presence of spoken words
  • Instrumentalness — Whether a track contains no vocals
  • Liveness — Likelihood of a live recording
  • Tempo — Beats per minute (BPM)
  • Loudness — Overall loudness in decibels
  • Key & Mode — Musical key and major/minor tonality
  • Duration — Length of the song in seconds
  • Popularity — Spotify's popularity score for the track

This dataset offers a rich view into the characteristics of popular music across Europe. By comparing these audio features country by country, we can explore whether geography and culture leave a measurable fingerprint on listening habits.
